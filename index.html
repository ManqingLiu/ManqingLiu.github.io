About me

I am a PhD student in Epidemiology at Harvard University advised by Dr. James Robins and Dr. Andrew Beam. My research interests are in the intersection of causal inference and machine learning.

Previously, I worked at Penn LDI as a biostatistician, during which I worked with LDI-affiliated investigators on health policy and population health research using nationwide healthcare dataset including Medicare & Medicaid data and Optum data.

The project which motivated me to pursue a PhD degree is about determining the long term trajectories of predictions of a machine learning model developed to predict mortaltiy among patients with cancer. Collaborating with Dr. Ravi Parikh and Dr. Jinbo Chen, we realized that predictions generated by machine learning models are not perfect, and how to incorporate uncertainty in the predictions with decision-making process of clinicians was still an unresolved problem (see our paper published in NPJ Digital Medicine here). I could not solve the problem at that time, and decided to study causal inference and machine learning in more depth with the hope that I might find the answer along the way.
