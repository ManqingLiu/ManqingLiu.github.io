About me

I am a PhD Candidate in Epidemiology (minor in computational science and engineering (CSE) and biostatistics) at Harvard University advised by Dr. Andrew Beam and Dr. James Robins . I am working on combining the strengths of machine learning and causal inference, targeting the elucidation of causation rather than mere correlations within data. This approach aims to yield unbiased causal estimates even in high-dimensional settings, circumventing challenges related to convergence and stringent model assumptions.

Previously, I worked at Penn LDI as a biostatistician, during which I worked with LDI-affiliated investigators on health policy and population health research using nationwide healthcare dataset including Medicare & Medicaid data and Optum data .
